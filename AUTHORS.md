# Authors

GEOSPH was originally developed by **Dr. Alomir H. Favero Neto** beginning in 2020 as a research software framework for Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) applied to computational geomechanics.

The software is built upon the open-source **PySPH** framework (https://github.com/pypr/pysph), extending it with numerical methods, constitutive models, algorithms, and capabilities developed specifically for geomechanics research.

Throughout its development, collaborators and students have contributed project-specific features and validation studies. These contributions are recognized in **CONTRIBUTORS.md**.

The development of GEOSPH continues as part of Dr. Favero's research program in computational geomechanics.

Authorship of the GEOSPH framework should be distinguished from authorship of scientific publications that use the software. Contributions to individual research projects do not necessarily imply authorship or co-development of the underlying software framework.
